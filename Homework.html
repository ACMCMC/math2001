  1. Let \(a\) be a real number and suppose that \(a ^ 3 = 0\). Show that \(a = 0\).

    You will probably use the lemma eq_zero_or_eq_zero_of_mul_eq_zero, stating that if \(ab=0\) then either \(a=0\) or \(b=0\).

    Note: Example 2.3.6 in the text is similar (but simpler), and trying it first will give you some ideas for this one. But don’t invoke the lemma pow_eq_zero mentioned in that example: the idea is that we’re establishing here a special case of that lemma.
